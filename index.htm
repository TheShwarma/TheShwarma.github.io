01 A Jew in Rome  by  Jewish History, 5B

 

You are a Jew who lives in Rome. Your ancestors came here as slaves,

captured by the Emperor Titus in the year 3830 (that is, the year 70 according to the way the Christians count it).

You have always enjoyed going to the synagogue, but last shabbat, you asked your rabbi …..

Your rabbi said…..

But you weren't satisfied with your rabbi's answer. Your rabbi suggests that if you want to learn how to find answers, you should  go and study.

"Where should I go?" you ask the rabbi. Your rabbi explains that there is really only one place to study, and that is Baghdad. It is Rosh Hodesh Shevat, and your rabbi says that people who want to study go to Baghdad for the month of Adar. It will take about two weeks to get to Baghdad, so you have to decide in two weeks.

You ask your parents….

You aren't sure about going to Baghdad. The non-Jews there are Muslims and you don't know how you would get along. Your cousin, however, has invited you to come north through Italy up to the Rhine valley where he is going to be a merchant in the new city of Speyer.
